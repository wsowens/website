About

Hey there! Welcome to my little piece of the internet.

I'm a recent graduate from the University of Florida. There, I studied computer science, biology, and everything in between. I received a B.S. in Computer Science (cum laude) and B.S. in Microbiology (summa cum laude).

I was honored to be named a Goldwater Scholar in 2019, an award given to those students show "the greatest potential" to become leaders in their field. Out of a graduating class of roughly 9,000 UF students, I was one of 9 named a Four-Year Outstanding Scholar.

I'm now a Synthetic Biology Apprentice at Octant, helping to revolutionize the way we approach drug discovery. As an SBA, I've been paired with top notch scientists (Octonauts) who teach me the latest synthetic biology and cell engineering techniques. In the future, I aspire to get a Ph.D. in Biological Engineering and help unlock the untapped potential of the cell.

Biology can help solve our most complex challenges.

Goals

  • Explore organisms & machines from a unified computational-biological perspective
  • Encourage more public engagement with science, mathematics, & philosophy
  • Leverage the power of biology to solve real world problems
